RATAN TATA

A major philanthropist, he has invested over 65 percent of his share in charitable trusts. A key goal of his life is to give Indians a better quality of life and ensure human development.

Early Life

Ratan Tata was born on 28th December 1937 to Naval Tata and Sonoo Tata. He has a brother Jimmy and a step-brother, Noel Tata. When Ratan Tata was 10 years old, his parents separated. Both the brothers were brought up by their grandmother Navajbai Tata. His early schooling was done in Mumbai at Campion School and John Connon School. He received his degree in Architecture with structural engineering from Cornell University, U.S.A. In 1975 he completed an advanced management programme at the Harvard Business school. He once loved a girl in Los Angeles while working there and had to return to India as his family member was ill. The girl's parents didn't allow her to go to India. Tata stood by his commitment and is unmarried to date.

Chair person - TATA

When JRD Tata stepped down as the chairperson of Tata Sons in 1991, he named Ratan Tata his successor. He faced stiff resistance from many companies heads who spent decades in their respective companies. Tata began replacing them by setting a retirement age. He further made it compulsory for each company to report to the group office. Under his leadership, the overlapping companies of Tata Sons were streamlined into a synergized whole.
During his 21 years of stewardship, revenues grew over 40 times, and profit over 50 times. He got Tata Tea to acquire Tetley, Tata Motors to acquire Jaguar Land Rover, and Tata Steel to acquire Corus, turning the organization from a largely India-centric group into a global business.

Philanthropist

Ratan Tata has significantly contributed to various charitable causes, including education, healthcare, and rural development.
He established the Tata Trusts, which oversee the distribution of billions of dollars towards improving the quality of life in India.
His philanthropy extends to funding institutions like Tata Institute of Social Sciences and Tata Memorial Hospital.
Ratan Tata has supported initiatives for clean drinking water, vocational training, and entrepreneurship.
He donated generously during national crises, such as natural disasters and the COVID-19 pandemic.
Tata has donated to disaster relief efforts, providing aid during natural calamities like the 2008 Mumbai attacks and the 2013 Uttarakhand floods.

Leading light

Ratan Tata, a beacon of integrity, vision, and compassion, has left an indelible mark on society through his exemplary leadership and unwavering commitment to philanthropy. His relentless pursuit of excellence, coupled with his deep sense of social responsibility, has inspired generations. From pioneering innovations to transformative philanthropic endeavors, Tata's legacy resonates far beyond boardrooms and spreadsheets. Today, as we reflect on his remarkable contributions, we extend our heartfelt gratitude for his selfless service and enduring impact. Thank you, Ratan Tata, for illuminating our path with your wisdom, generosity, and boundless compassion.
